Research output

Publications

My research sits at the intersection of developmental musculoskeletal biology and applied sports science. I study how the growing skeleton responds to athletic load — and what happens when that response goes wrong. Apophyseal pathologies in youth athletes are my primary focus: conditions that are common, consequential, and almost entirely without evidence-based treatment. In parallel, I investigate the broader landscape of youth athlete development — maturation, movement quality, specialization, and asymmetry — because understanding who is at risk, and why, is inseparable from knowing how to help them.

— Bartosz Wilczyński

Wilczyński B, Buler J, Buler R, Grochowski M, Puchalski B, Ramirez-Campillo R, Gepfert M, Zorena K
